# Step 1 — Parts Inventory

**Open:** [`01-parts-inventory.scad`](01-parts-inventory.scad) in OpenSCAD, press **F5**.

Before you drill anything or glue anything, lay all 15 cut pieces out on the floor or a workbench so you can see them at a glance. This is the woodworking version of dumping the LEGO bag onto the table and sorting before you start.

## What you should have in front of you

| Group | Qty | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Legs | 4 | 32.25" long | The tallest pieces. Run full height of the stand. |
| Front/back rails | 4 | 9" long | The **shortest** 2×4 pieces. These will get pocket holes in the next step. |
| Left/right rails | 4 | 13" long | Slightly longer than the F/B rails. No pocket holes — they get screwed through the leg face. |
| Plywood squares | 3 | 16" × 16" × ¾" | Top, shelf, and base board. All identical for now — you'll notch one of them later. |

Three of the 2×4 pieces look almost the same (the 9" and 13" rails are close in length), which is exactly why sorting them now matters. **Put the 9s in one pile and the 13s in another.** If you mix them up mid-assembly you'll waste time re-measuring.

## Quick sanity checks

- [ ] Count: 4 + 4 + 4 + 3 = **15 pieces** total
- [ ] All four legs are the same length — stack them end-to-end and check that the ends line up. Any that are more than ~1/16" off, trim to match the shortest one.
- [ ] All four 9" rails match each other. Same check.
- [ ] All four 13" rails match each other. Same check.
- [ ] The three plywood squares are actually square (check with a framing square, or measure both diagonals — they should be equal).
- [ ] No cracks running along the grain near the ends of the 2×4s — if you see one, swap that piece out.

## Why matching lengths matters

If one leg is 1/8" shorter than the others, the finished stand will wobble on a hard floor. (On carpet it won't matter much, since the base board and the carpet itself absorb small differences — but matching now is free, and re-cutting later is not.)

Matching rail pairs is even more important: if your two front rails are different lengths, the frame can't sit square, and the whole stand will lean.

## When you're done

You should be looking at something that resembles the SCAD preview: four neat piles of lumber plus three plywood squares, all accounted for. **Say "ready" and I'll give you Step 2** (drilling pocket holes in the 9" rails).
