# Water Chemistry — Preliminary Notes

Target species: Neocaridina shrimp

## Key Parameters to Manage
- **NH3 (ammonia)** — lethal at low concentrations, managed by biological filtration (substrate + filter)
- **NO2 (nitrite)** — also toxic, intermediate in nitrogen cycle
- **NO3 (nitrate)** — less toxic but high levels stress shrimp; managed by plants + water changes
- **CO2** — no pressurized injection planned; natural equilibrium from shrimp respiration + surface agitation
- **PO4 (phosphate)** — fuels algae; some biofilm growth is desirable for shrimp grazing, excess causes blooms
- **pH** — neocaridina prefer ~6.5–7.5; CO2 and substrate buffer this
- **GH / KH** — general and carbonate hardness; neocaridina need adequate minerals for molting

## Lighting & Algae Balance
- Goal: enough light for beneficial biofilm (shrimp food) without triggering nuisance algae
- SEAOURA clip-on light — will need to dial in photoperiod (likely 6–8 hrs/day to start)
